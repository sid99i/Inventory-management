Why Inventory Management

Inventory refers to either saleable products or the raw materials available in hand for manufacturing products for sale, whereas management is a distinct process consisting of planning, organising, actuating and controlling/utilising the available resources to achieve predetermined objectives. Merging the two we get Inventory management; Inventory management is essential for tracking inventory from suppliers to warehouses and lastly to customers. Without proper management for a corporate organisation, inventory would either be available in excess, or there would be a crisis for the same.

Objectives of Inventory Management

  • Predicting the best product for both consumer and supplier.
  • Increasing the sales by predicting the demand of the products.
  • Creating a supply and demand balance for products.
  • Updation of seller’s inventory according to the provided budget.
